# Sprint 1 World Bible

## Working Title

**The Architect's Path**

## Core Premise

The visitor is not browsing a portfolio. They are entering a civilization built around the act of organizing chaos into systems that help people. Jeff's professional identity is translated into a world of archives, forges, citadels, observatories, AI sanctums, and living workshops.

## First Age

Before civilization learned to govern knowledge, there was only chaos: records without context, tools without purpose, predictions without accountability, and decisions without memory. The Architects were not kings. They were builders who discovered that structure itself could become a civic technology.

## Player Role

The visitor is called **the Seeker**. They are not told Jeff's resume. They uncover it through action.

## Companion

**Corvus** is an ancient raven-machine: brass feathers, blue eyes, dry humor, and a memory older than the visible world. He never dumps information. He comments, questions, and occasionally challenges the player.

## Six Realms

### The Forgotten Archive
Healthcare analytics, Epic, Clarity, reporting, patient-centered data work.

### The Forge of Systems
Fabric, Databricks, dbt, Fivetran, orchestration, governed data movement.

### The Citadel
Leadership, governance, value stream pods, manager coaching, organizational design.

### The Hall of Intelligence
AI adoption, LLMs, agents, model governance, ThreadlineAI.

### The Observatory of Possibilities
Research, quantum computing curiosity, simulation, weather experiments, future systems.

### The Living Workshop
GitHub projects as living artifacts that change as the real repositories change.

## Stone Tablets

The world contains leadership philosophy as environmental lore. Sprint 1 ships the first six tablets.

1. The strongest wall is not built from stone. It is built from understanding.
2. Every system eventually reflects those who designed it.
3. A leader who hoards knowledge has already begun to fail.
4. Dashboards matter less than the decisions they improve.
5. Prediction is not the goal. Prevention is the goal.
6. The work is not to impress the machine. The work is to help people move.
